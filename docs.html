INTRODUCTION

Welcome to the Plano West NHS Documentation. This page hosts all relevant information for PWNHS members regarding the club and its operations.

What is PWNHS?

Plano West NHS is the most monumental service organization in the greater DFW area. We are devoted to aiding the community and to exemplifying what it means to be an honor student. You can check out our Four Pillars for more information on NHS foci.

Why Should I Care About PWNHS?

The National Honor Society offers many benefits, both for your social and academic experience at West. Some of these benefits may be found here. Plus, it never hurts to say you were part of the nation's premier high school organization!


GENERAL CONSIDERATIONS


Volunteer Hours

Members must complete 15 hours of volunteer service each semester to remain in good standing with the Plano West NHS. Be sure that your NHS hours come from approved organizations and that you abide by our restrictions.

Penalty Hours

They accumulate quickly! 2 penalty hours are served for an absence at a mandatory meeting, missed + 5 hours are required for every semester you don't complete 15 hours. Refrain from ever getting penalty hours, and complete them quickly if you do. Our penalty hours tab dives into more detail.

Obligations

Make sure to observe honorable behavior. Violation of the law, the school conduct code, or academic integrity will result in immediate expulsion. Review your obligations here.

VOLUNTEER HOURS

Volunteering is a huge part of NHS. Your ability to aid the community around you goes a long way to demonstrating your capabilities as an individual. We're sure that you will do many amazing things during your time proffered this year.

Hours Sheet

You can download the PWNHS volunteer hours sheet next to this block.Download Hours Sheet (.docx)

Guidelines for Hours

Please keep in mind that when filling out your sheet, there should be no crossing out or rewriting of hours, names, or signatures - this is required to eliminate suspicion of fraud. If you must cross out a parameter, cross out the entire box and start another one.

All members must complete 15 hours volunteer hours each semester. No double dipping! Hours turned in for NHS do not count for another club, and vice versa; we do cross-check.

All fall hours must be turned in by Dec. 1.

Restrictions

Up to 10 hours from the summer may count towards NHS, and may only be redeemed during the fall semester.

Up to 10 hours from one organization may count towards NHS. The rest must be from other sources.

Don't wear yourself out! You may only volunteer 8 hours at a time (at one organization on one day).

Only 5 hours may be from PTSA/Booster Club events. In addition, these events must be approved by the PWNHS. Approval is done on a case-by-case, rolling basis, so contact us as soon as possible if you think you may want to use one of these events for your hours!

The organizations you volunteer at must be sanctioned as "approved" by either the national branch of the NHS or the local PWNHS. Don't know if your organization is approved?

As unfortunate as it may be, only secular participation for approved organizations is accepted for NHS hours. In other words,no religiously-afilliated volunteering may be used for your NHS volunteer hours. This includes, but is not limited to, church/mosque/temple events, religious charity, and the like. However, if a community event is held at one of these locations or they are aiding the community without promoting the religious or political view, volunteer hours are acceptable. For example: Vacation Bible Schools would not be allowed but a food pantry at the same church would be allowed.

Donation does not count as volunteering. Members may not recieve hours for donating items or money, but they can receive hours for helping to sort donations at a collection site or helping organize and assist during a fundraiser.

Students who are part of any organization and volunteer at an event the organization is sponsoring may receive volunteer hours if they are not required by the organization to attend, or if it goes above and beyond their requirements. This includes some organizations at West, Eagle Scouts, Boy and Girl Scouts.

Helping teachers WILL NOT COUNT FOR NHS HOURS.

Relationships

The Plano West NHS encourages you to build a lasting, concerted relationship with one or more of the organizations you volunteer at and to give back to your community on a regular basis. It looks great, and makes you feel even better!

PENALTY HOURS

Sometimes you can't make two meetings or aren't able to finish all your hours. That's okay! But to be fair to all of our members and adhere to NHS guidelines, we must give penalty hours to all those who are not able to fulfill PWNHS requirements.

What Incites Penalty Hours?

Failure to complete the 15 required volunteer hours each semester will result in penalty hours for the next semester. The number of penalty hours will be defined as the number of uncompleted hours + 5. For instance, if you only finish 12 hours one semester, you will have missed 3 and have to make up 3 + 5 = 8 hours the next semester in addition to the normal, required 15 hours per semester (i.e. you will have to do 15 + 8 = 23 hours in total for the next semester).

You are required to attend two of four PWNHS meetings each semester. Failure to miss one required meeting will result in 2 penalty hours. Failure to miss both required meetings will result in 4 penalty hours. Penalty hours for missed meetings must be turned in the same semester they were dealt.

5 penalty hours will be served to those who do not submit their Obligation Form or Dues by September 21, or who do not submit their fall hours by 4:30 PM, December 1.

DEADLINES ARE EXTREMELY STRICT!

Completing Penalty Hours

This year, we are asking our members to turn in their regular volunteer hours and penalty hours jointly. Therefore, the penalty hours sheet is the same as the regular hours sheet.

Turn in your penalty hours sheet(s) together with your regular hours sheets by the required due date(s). Your completion of penalty hours must abide by the same guidelines and restrictions as your regular volunteer hours.

MEETINGS

Meetings are our primary opportunity to interact with the PWNHS community and provide you with updates on the club and our incessant progress. They may be big, but they hold a lot of essential information.

General

Meetings are held on the following specified dates during A/B Lunch. Other important dates, including due dates, are included:


  • Wed, August 31 - Senior Informational Meeting
  • Thur, September 1 - Junior Informational Meeting
  • Tues, September 13 - Growth Meeting for members who plan to participate in: Plano Balloon Festival, PWSH Homecoming Parade Cleanup, PWSH Open House Volunteers, or Turkey Trot
  • Wed, September 21 - Obligation forms, annual dues, and t-shirt orders due by 4:30 PM.
  • Tues, October 11 - Senior Growth Meeting with Guest Speaker
  • Tues, October 18 - Junior Growth Meeting with Guest Speaker
  • Tues, November 1 - Catch-all Final Fall Growth Meeting
  • Thur, December 1 - Fall Volunteer Hours due by 4:30 PM

Of the four meetings each semester, you are required to attend two. During the fall, you must attend the first meeting (informational) and one of the other three meetings (growth).

Miss a Meeting?

If you miss a meeting, don't fret! Attend another one so that you can get the two you need.

Try not to miss our informational meeting, as it is the keynote meeting of the year. If you are unlucky enough to be unable to make it, make up the meeting by attending two growth meetings. Or, you can just do two penalty hours in addition to the required 15 regular hours.

MEMBER OBLIGATIONS

The Plano West NHS strives to maintain an active, interconnected, and virtuous community of members. In order to achieve this ever-continuous goal, we require our members to commit themselves to some basic obligations.

Obligation Form

You can download the Obligation Form next to this block. Download Obligations (.docx)

All PWNHS members must submit this form following the introductory (first) meeting of the fall semester, alongside their $6 dues, by September 21 to Room 2013.

Membership Qualifications

To become a member of the Plano West NHS, you must have attended Plano West, Jasper, or Shepton for a semester at minimum. New candidates to the PWNHS will be reviewed and approved by a majority vote of the faculty council before induction. New candidates are required to attend induction in order to achieve standing as official members.

Note: If you were a part of NHS your sophmore year at Jasper/Shepton, or part of NHS your junior year at West, you are automatically accepted into PWNHS. Seniors who were not in NHS their junior year cannot join PWNHS. If you wish to join NHS and were not a member in 10th grade, you must do so at the start of your junior year. The national guidelines state that members must be inducted prior to their senior year. Juniors go through the probationary period in the fall and are inducted in the spring. No one can decide in January that he or she would like to join.

Transfer Students

Transfer students must bring a letter of good standing from their previous principal or NHS chapter advisor, at which point they will be automatically accepted as a member of the PWNHS. Jasper/Shepton members are not transfer students.

Membership Requirements

All PWNHS members must have a minimum 3.60 GPA. No rounding, no exceptions!

Members are expected to contribute $6 in membership dues at the start of the year. These dues help fund national membership fees, special events and projects, meeting supplies, guest speakers, and internal PWNHS ventures. Dues must be submitted with the obligation form by September 21 to Room 2013. T-Shirts are not included in the membership dues, and will be sold starting September. T-Shirt orders will also be due September 21.

Members are expected to complete 15 hours of community service per semester. These hours may not be counted for or used for any other purpose. 10 summer hours may be applied to the fall semester only. All fall semester hours must be turned in by December 1th to room 2013. More information on hours can be found here.

Members must attend 2 meetings per semester. Read up on PWNHS meetings here.

Members must not violate the law, the school conduct code, or academic integrity in order to remain in good standing with the club. Information on consequences of such actions may be found below.

Discipline and Dismissal Policy

Violations of the law, the school conduct code, or academic integrity will result in probation, additional hours, or dismissal as decided and enforced by the PWNHS core / faculty council on a case-by-case basis.

If the PWNHS suggests the dismissal of a member, the condition will be decided by the faculty council. A majority vote (Â½ + 1) is required for the motion of dismissal to pass. No veto is enabled.

To resign one's position as a PWNHS/NHS member, he or she must directly contact the PWNHS core.

Members who resign or are dismissed may not rejoin the PWNHS at a later time.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS


Do summer volunteer hours count towards NHS?

Yes, up to ten hours from the summer can be used for the fall semester.

If I do more than fifteen hours in one semester, can they rollover to the next?

Although it's amazing that you did more than fifteen hours, and we always encourage our members to go above and beyond, no. We want to see that you consistently contribute to your community throughout the entire year. Still amazing though.

When do I have to finish penalty hours?

Penalty hours must be completed and submitted the same semester they are served.

How long will it take for my organization to be approved?

One week, but it must be submitted two weeks in advance of your planned volunteer date.

What about national awards, like the Gold Volunteer Award?

Don't worry, we're working on it!

When will officer applications be released?

About halfway through February.
